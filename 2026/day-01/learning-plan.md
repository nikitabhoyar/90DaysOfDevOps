 ** 90-day personal DevOps Learning plan**

 1) What is your understanding of DevOps and Cloud Engineering ?

 - My understanding of DevOps is a modern approach of software development that brings development and operations team together to deliver application faster and more reliable.
 - It encourages close collaboration between development and operations teams.
 - It automates build, test and deployment process to reduce errors. 
 - Enable faster and more frequent software releases.
 - Improves system reliability, monitoring and feedback loops.

 - Coming to Cloud Engineering is the practise of designing, building and managing scalable,secure and reliable virtual infrastructure and applications on Cloud Platform like AWS,GCP,Azure.

 2) Why you are starting learning DevOps & Cloud ?

- Starting to learn DevOps and Cloud in 2026 is driven by industry's shift toward automation, the rise of AI driven infrastructure, and the massive demand for professionals who can bridge the gap between development and operations.

3) Where do you want to reach ?

- In my career, I want to grow into a highly skilled Cloud DevOps Engineer and eventually move toward a DevOps Architect or Cloud Infrastructure leadership role.

In the next 2–3 years, my focus is to strengthen my expertise in AWS, Kubernetes, CI/CD automation, Infrastructure as Code, monitoring, and cloud security by working on large-scale production environments.

Long term, I want to contribute to designing scalable, secure, and automated cloud infrastructure solutions that help organizations deploy faster and more reliably. I also want to mentor junior engineers and contribute to building strong engineering practices within a team.

Right now, my immediate goal is to join a product-based company where I can work on real-world cloud infrastructure challenges, learn from experienced teams, and continuously grow as a DevOps professional.

4) How you will stay consistent every single day ?

- I stay consistent by setting clear daily goals and following a structured learning and work routine.

I break larger goals into smaller achievable tasks—for example, if I’m learning Kubernetes, I divide it into concepts, hands-on practice, documentation, and implementation. This helps me make measurable progress every day instead of feeling overwhelmed.

I also track my progress regularly through notes, documentation, and practical projects. Consistency for me comes from discipline and continuous improvement rather than waiting for motivation.

In technology, learning is continuous, so I make it a habit to dedicate time every day to strengthening my technical skills through hands-on practice and real-world implementation.

5) Define **3 clear goals** for the next 90 days.

- Over the next 90 days, my focus is on three things: first, sharpening my AWS and Kubernetes practical skills through projects; second, strengthening my automation expertise with CI/CD and Infrastructure as Code tools; and third, transitioning into a strong DevOps role where I can contribute while continuing to grow technically.
